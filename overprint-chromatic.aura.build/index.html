02 The separation

One word.
Four passes.

Every glyph is drawn as four overlapping plates. Stack them in register and you read one clean impression. Pull them apart and the letter splits into light, the way a press drifts on a Monday morning. Drag the control to take it apart yourself.

The argument

Most type treats color as a finish you add later. Overprint treats color as structure. Every letter is four plates, so a headline can break apart, drift, and find its register in front of the reader. Ink is not decoration. It is the letter.
